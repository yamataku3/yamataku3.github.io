abstract: >
  Inappropriate holding postures and motions can negatively affect the
  formation of attachment, which is the bonding between caregivers and
  children, and can cause head injuries. Therefore, first-time caregivers
  need to learn appropriate neonate holding. In this study, we propose a
  smartphone and stuffed animal-based system for teaching neonates holding.
  The system trains the appropriate inclination angle, adduction angle, and
  acceleration value based on the holding motions observed in 15 midwives.
  The user attaches a smartphone to a stuffed animal and practices the
  motions with it. Then the system evaluates its movements and gives
  real-time feedback. We conducted a user study (N = 42) to evaluate the
  effectiveness of this system. We found that the system was effective in
  learning the inclination angle.
